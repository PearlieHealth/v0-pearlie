Choosing between Invisalign and traditional braces is one of the most common decisions patients face when considering teeth straightening. Both options can deliver excellent results, but they differ in cost, comfort, visibility, and how they fit into your daily life.

This guide compares Invisalign and braces across every factor that matters, so you can make an informed choice.

## Quick comparison

| Factor | Invisalign | Traditional Braces |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Cost | £2,500 – £5,500 | £1,500 – £3,500 |
| Treatment time | 6 – 18 months | 12 – 24 months |
| Visibility | Nearly invisible | Visible (metal) or ceramic |
| Removable | Yes | No |
| Diet restrictions | None (remove to eat) | Yes (avoid hard/sticky foods) |
| Maintenance | Easy (remove to brush) | More difficult |
| Best for | Mild to moderate cases | All complexity levels |

## How Invisalign works

Invisalign uses a series of custom-made, clear plastic aligners that gradually move your teeth into the desired position. You wear each set of aligners for 1–2 weeks before switching to the next set in the series.

Key points about Invisalign:

- **Removable** — take them out to eat, drink, and brush your teeth
- **Nearly invisible** — most people won't notice you're wearing them
- **Comfortable** — smooth plastic with no wires or brackets
- **Requires discipline** — you need to wear them 20–22 hours per day for best results
- **Regular check-ups** — visits every 6–8 weeks to track progress

## How traditional braces work

Traditional braces use metal or ceramic brackets bonded to your teeth, connected by a wire that's periodically tightened to move teeth gradually. Modern braces are smaller and more comfortable than older versions.

Key points about braces:

- **Fixed** — they work 24/7 without needing patient compliance
- **Effective for complex cases** — can treat severe crowding, bite issues, and complex tooth movements
- **Visible** — though ceramic (tooth-coloured) options are less noticeable
- **Adjustments needed** — visits every 4–6 weeks for wire tightening
- **Dietary restrictions** — avoid hard, sticky, and crunchy foods

## Cost comparison in the UK

### Invisalign costs

- **Invisalign Lite** (mild cases): £2,500 – £3,500
- **Invisalign Comprehensive** (moderate cases): £3,500 – £5,500
- **Invisalign Teen**: £3,000 – £4,500

### Braces costs

- **Metal braces**: £1,500 – £3,000
- **Ceramic braces**: £2,000 – £3,500
- **Lingual braces** (behind teeth): £3,000 – £8,000

Both treatments are typically **not available on the NHS for adults** — orthodontic treatment on the NHS is generally only available for children and young people with a clinical need.

## Which is better for your case?

### Choose Invisalign if:

- You want a discreet treatment option
- Your case is mild to moderate (gaps, minor crowding, slight bite issues)
- You're disciplined about wearing aligners consistently
- You don't want dietary restrictions
- Appearance during treatment matters to you

### Choose braces if:

- You have a complex orthodontic case (severe crowding, significant bite correction)
- You want a "set it and forget it" approach (no risk of forgetting to wear aligners)
- Budget is a primary concern (metal braces are usually cheaper)
- Your orthodontist recommends braces for clinical reasons

## Effectiveness: can Invisalign do everything braces can?

Invisalign has improved significantly and can now treat most cases that braces can, including:

- Crowding and spacing
- Overbite, underbite, and crossbite
- Open bite
- Mild to moderate rotation

However, braces still have an edge for **severe** orthodontic cases, complex tooth rotations, and cases requiring significant vertical movement. Your orthodontist will advise if your case is suitable for Invisalign.

## How to decide

The best way to determine which option is right for you is to consult with an experienced orthodontist or dentist who offers both treatments. A good provider will recommend the option best suited to your specific case rather than pushing one treatment over another.

> **Pearlie tip:** When comparing clinics, ask if the dentist is an Invisalign provider and what their experience level is. Invisalign providers are rated by tiers (Bronze to Diamond+) based on the number of cases they've treated.
