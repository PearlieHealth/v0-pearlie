Dental implants are widely considered the gold standard for replacing missing teeth — but how much do they actually cost in the UK? Whether you're considering a single implant or a full mouth restoration, understanding the pricing landscape helps you make an informed decision.

In this guide, we break down the real costs of dental implants across the UK in 2026, what affects pricing, and how to find a qualified implant dentist.

## How much does a single dental implant cost?

A single dental implant in the UK typically costs between **£2,000 and £3,500**. This usually includes the implant fixture (the titanium post), the abutment (connector piece), and the crown.

| Component | Typical Cost |
|-----------|-------------|
| Implant fixture (titanium post) | £800 – £1,500 |
| Abutment (connector) | £200 – £500 |
| Crown (porcelain or zirconia) | £500 – £1,200 |
| **Total single implant** | **£2,000 – £3,500** |

Prices vary significantly depending on the clinic's location, the implant brand used, and the complexity of your case.

## What affects dental implant prices?

Several factors influence the final cost of your dental implant treatment:

### Clinic location

London clinics tend to charge 20–40% more than clinics outside the capital. A single implant in central London might cost £3,000–£3,500, while the same treatment in Manchester or Birmingham could be £2,000–£2,500.

### Implant brand and materials

Premium implant systems like Straumann and Nobel Biocare cost more than budget alternatives, but they come with longer track records and better clinical evidence. The crown material also matters — zirconia crowns are typically more expensive than porcelain-fused-to-metal.

### Bone grafting and additional procedures

If you've had missing teeth for a while, you may need bone grafting before implant placement. This can add **£400–£1,500** to the overall cost depending on the extent of grafting required.

### The dentist's experience

Implant placement is a skilled procedure. Dentists with advanced implant training, fellowships, or decades of experience often charge more — but the investment in expertise can mean better outcomes.

## Full mouth dental implant costs

For patients needing to replace all teeth, there are several options:

| Treatment | Typical Cost | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| All-on-4 (per arch) | £8,000 – £15,000 | 4 implants supporting a full arch of teeth |
| All-on-6 (per arch) | £10,000 – £18,000 | 6 implants for added stability |
| Full mouth (both arches) | £16,000 – £30,000 | Complete upper and lower restoration |
| Individual implants (full mouth) | £25,000 – £50,000+ | Each tooth replaced individually |

The **All-on-4** technique has become increasingly popular because it provides a full set of fixed teeth using just four implants per arch, making it more affordable than replacing each tooth individually.

## Are dental implants available on the NHS?

In most cases, dental implants are **not available on the NHS**. The NHS considers implants a cosmetic or elective treatment and only covers them in exceptional circumstances, such as:

- Patients who cannot wear conventional dentures due to severe bone loss
- Reconstruction after oral cancer treatment
- Trauma cases referred by a hospital consultant

For the vast majority of patients, dental implants are a private treatment. However, many clinics offer **payment plans** to spread the cost over 12–60 months.

## How to find the right implant dentist

When choosing a dentist for implants, look for:

- **GDC registration** — all dentists in the UK must be registered with the General Dental Council
- **Implant-specific qualifications** — look for diplomas or fellowships in implantology (e.g., from the Royal College of Surgeons)
- **Before-and-after photos** — ask to see examples of previous implant cases
- **Transparent pricing** — a good clinic will provide a detailed treatment plan with clear costs before you commit
- **Patient reviews** — read what other implant patients have experienced

> **Pearlie tip:** Don't choose based on price alone. The cheapest option isn't always the best value — consider the dentist's experience, the implant brand used, and what's included in the quoted price.

## Payment options for dental implants

Most UK dental clinics offer flexible payment options for implants:

- **Interest-free finance** — spread the cost over 6–12 months with 0% APR
- **Extended payment plans** — longer terms (24–60 months) with interest
- **Dental insurance** — some plans cover part of the implant cost (check your policy)
- **Health cash plans** — providers like Denplan may contribute toward treatment

Always ask your clinic about available finance options before committing to treatment.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, dental implants can last **20 years or more**. The titanium post itself can last a lifetime, though the crown may need replacing after 10–15 years due to normal wear.

### Is the implant procedure painful?

Implant placement is done under local anaesthetic, so you shouldn't feel pain during the procedure. Some discomfort and swelling is normal for a few days afterwards, manageable with over-the-counter painkillers.

### How long does the implant process take?

From initial consultation to final crown, the process typically takes **3–6 months**. This includes time for the implant to fuse with the jawbone (osseointegration). Same-day implant options are available at some clinics for suitable candidates.

### Can smokers have dental implants?

Smoking significantly increases the risk of implant failure. Most dentists will advise you to stop smoking before and after the procedure. Some clinics may decline to place implants in heavy smokers.
