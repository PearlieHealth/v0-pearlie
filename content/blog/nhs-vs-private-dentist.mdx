One of the most common questions patients in the UK face is whether to go NHS or private for dental care. Both have their place, and the right choice depends on your needs, budget, and what treatments you're looking for.

This guide breaks down the key differences to help you decide.

## NHS dental charges at a glance

NHS dental treatment in England is divided into three charge bands:

| Band | Cost (2026) | What's included |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Band 1 | £26.80 | Check-up, diagnosis, X-rays, scale and polish, prevention advice |
| Band 2 | £73.50 | Everything in Band 1 + fillings, root canal, extractions |
| Band 3 | £319.10 | Everything in Band 2 + crowns, dentures, bridges |

**Note:** NHS charges differ in Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland. In Scotland, NHS dental check-ups are free for all adults.

## Key differences

### Treatments available

- **NHS:** Covers clinically necessary treatments only. Cosmetic treatments like teeth whitening, veneers, and Invisalign are not available
- **Private:** Full range of treatments including cosmetic, implants, and advanced orthodontics

### Materials used

- **NHS:** Functional materials that meet clinical standards (e.g., amalgam fillings, basic dentures)
- **Private:** Choice of premium materials (e.g., white composite fillings, porcelain crowns, zirconia implants)

### Appointment availability

- **NHS:** Often long waiting times. Many areas of the UK have no NHS dentists accepting new patients
- **Private:** Generally shorter waiting times and more flexible scheduling

### Time per appointment

- **NHS:** Appointments tend to be shorter due to contract pressures
- **Private:** Typically longer, more thorough appointments

### Continuity of care

- **NHS:** You may see different dentists at the same practice
- **Private:** More likely to see the same dentist consistently

## When NHS dental care makes sense

NHS dental care is a good choice if:

- You need routine check-ups and basic treatments
- Budget is your primary concern
- You qualify for free NHS dental treatment (under 18, pregnant, on certain benefits)
- You need emergency dental care and don't have a private dentist

## When private dental care makes sense

Private dental care is worth considering if:

- You want cosmetic treatments (whitening, veneers, Invisalign)
- You need dental implants
- You can't find an NHS dentist accepting new patients in your area
- You want more time with your dentist and a wider choice of materials
- You prefer a specific dentist and want continuity of care

## Can I mix NHS and private treatment?

Yes, but not within the same course of treatment. You can be registered with an NHS dentist for routine care and see a private dentist for specific treatments like implants or cosmetic work. Many patients take this hybrid approach.

> **Important:** An NHS dentist cannot charge you privately for a treatment that's available on the NHS within the same course of treatment. If a treatment is clinically necessary, it should be offered at NHS rates.

## Finding an NHS dentist

Finding an NHS dentist accepting new patients can be challenging, especially in parts of England. Options include:

- **NHS website:** Use the "Find a dentist" tool at nhs.uk
- **Call NHS 111:** They can help locate practices accepting patients
- **Local Healthwatch:** Your local Healthwatch can advise on dental access in your area
