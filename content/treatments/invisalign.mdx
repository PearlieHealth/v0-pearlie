## What is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a teeth-straightening system that uses a series of custom-made, clear plastic aligners to gradually move your teeth into the correct position. Unlike traditional metal braces, the aligners are virtually invisible and can be removed for eating and brushing.

Each set of aligners is worn for 1–2 weeks before moving to the next set in the series. The entire treatment is planned digitally using 3D scans of your teeth, so you can see your projected results before you start.

Invisalign is the most widely recognised clear aligner brand in the world. It's manufactured by Align Technology and has been used to treat over 14 million patients globally.

## How Invisalign works

The Invisalign process typically involves five stages:

**1. Consultation and scanning**
Your dentist takes digital 3D scans of your teeth using an iTero scanner (no messy impressions). These scans are used to create your personalised treatment plan, including a digital preview of how your teeth will move at each stage.

**2. Custom aligner fabrication**
Your aligners are manufactured at an Invisalign facility using medical-grade SmartTrack plastic. Each aligner is precisely calibrated to apply gentle force to specific teeth.

**3. Wearing your aligners**
You wear each set of aligners for 20–22 hours per day, removing them only to eat, drink (anything other than water), brush, and floss. Most patients switch to a new set every 1–2 weeks.

**4. Progress check-ups**
You visit your dentist every 6–8 weeks to monitor progress and collect your next sets of aligners. Some cases also use small tooth-coloured attachments bonded to the teeth to help with more complex movements.

**5. Refinement and retention**
If needed, additional refinement aligners are ordered to perfect your results. Once treatment is complete, you transition to retainers (fixed, removable, or both) to maintain your new smile.

## How much does Invisalign cost in the UK?

Invisalign pricing in the UK depends on the complexity of your case and your location. Here's a breakdown of the main treatment tiers:

| Treatment tier | Complexity | Typical UK cost |
|---------------|-----------|----------------|
| Invisalign Express (Go) | Very mild (up to 7 aligners) | £1,500 – £2,500 |
| Invisalign Lite | Mild (up to 14 aligners) | £2,500 – £3,500 |
| Invisalign Moderate | Moderate (up to 26 aligners) | £3,000 – £4,500 |
| Invisalign Comprehensive | Complex (unlimited aligners) | £3,500 – £5,500 |

Central London clinics typically charge 20–30% more than practices outside the M25. The premium reflects higher operating costs, not necessarily different quality of care.

## Who is a good candidate for Invisalign?

Invisalign can treat a wide range of alignment issues, including:

- **Crowding** — teeth that overlap or are too close together
- **Spacing** — gaps between teeth
- **Overbite** — upper teeth protrude over lower teeth
- **Underbite** — lower teeth sit in front of upper teeth
- **Crossbite** — some upper teeth sit inside lower teeth
- **Open bite** — front teeth don't meet when biting down

Invisalign may not be suitable for very severe orthodontic cases, significant jaw misalignment, or patients who cannot commit to wearing aligners 20–22 hours per day. A consultation with a certified Invisalign provider is the best way to determine if you're a candidate.

## Invisalign vs traditional braces

| Factor | Invisalign | Traditional braces |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Visibility | Nearly invisible | Visible metal or ceramic brackets |
| Removable | Yes — for eating and brushing | No — fixed throughout treatment |
| Comfort | Smooth plastic, less irritation | Brackets and wires can irritate cheeks |
| Treatment time | 6–18 months (mild–complex) | 12–24 months (mild–complex) |
| Food restrictions | None (aligners removed to eat) | Avoid hard, sticky, and chewy foods |
| Cost (UK) | £2,500 – £5,500 | £1,500 – £6,000 |
| Check-up frequency | Every 6–8 weeks | Every 4–6 weeks |
| Best for | Mild to moderate cases, adults | Complex cases, all age groups |

Both options can deliver excellent results. Invisalign's main advantages are aesthetics and convenience. Traditional braces may be more effective for complex cases and are sometimes more affordable.

## What to expect during treatment

**Week 1:** Your aligners may feel tight and you might experience mild discomfort. Speech may feel slightly different for the first few days — this is normal and resolves quickly.

**Weeks 2–4:** You settle into a routine of wearing, removing, cleaning, and switching aligners. Most patients adapt completely within the first month.

**Months 2–6:** You begin to notice visible changes. Regular check-ups with your dentist ensure everything is progressing as planned.

**Final months:** Refinement aligners may be needed for final adjustments. Your dentist takes new scans and orders additional aligners if necessary.

**After treatment:** Retainers are essential. Most dentists recommend wearing a removable retainer every night indefinitely and may also place a thin bonded wire retainer behind your front teeth.

## How to choose the right Invisalign provider

When comparing Invisalign clinics, look for:

- **Invisalign provider tier** — Providers are ranked by volume: Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, and Diamond. Higher tiers indicate more experience with Invisalign cases.
- **GDC registration** — All dentists in the UK must be registered with the General Dental Council.
- **Before-and-after photos** — Ask to see results from cases similar to yours.
- **Transparent pricing** — The quote should include all aligners, attachments, retainers, and follow-up visits.
- **Patient reviews** — Look for consistent positive feedback about Invisalign specifically, not just general dentistry.
- **Consultation quality** — A good provider will take time to explain your treatment plan, timeline, and expected results using 3D visualisation.
