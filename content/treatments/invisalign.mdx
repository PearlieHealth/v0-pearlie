## What is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a teeth straightening system that uses a series of custom made, clear aligners to gradually move your teeth into the correct position.

Unlike traditional braces:

- **Nearly invisible.** Clear plastic aligners that most people won't notice
- **Removable.** Take them out for eating and brushing
- **Digitally planned.** 3D scanning maps your entire treatment before you begin

Your treatment is planned from start to finish using digital scans, so you can preview your projected results before committing.

Invisalign is manufactured by Align Technology and has been used to treat over 14 million patients worldwide.

## How Invisalign works

**1. Consultation and 3D scan**
Digital scans using an iTero scanner (no messy impressions) create your personalised treatment plan and a preview of how your teeth will move at each stage.

**2. Custom aligner fabrication**
Aligners are manufactured using medical grade SmartTrack material. Each aligner is precisely calibrated for controlled tooth movement.

**3. Wearing your aligners**
You wear each set for 20 to 22 hours per day, removing them only to eat, drink, brush, and floss. Most patients switch to a new set every one to two weeks.

**4. Progress reviews**
Regular check ups every six to eight weeks ensure teeth are moving correctly. Some cases use small tooth coloured attachments to assist with complex movements.

**5. Retention**
After treatment, retainers (fixed, removable, or both) are worn to maintain your results long term.

## Who is a good candidate?

Invisalign can treat a wide range of alignment issues:

- **Crowding.** Teeth that overlap or are too close together
- **Spacing.** Gaps between teeth
- **Overbite.** Upper teeth protrude over lower teeth
- **Underbite.** Lower teeth sit in front of upper teeth
- **Crossbite.** Some upper teeth sit inside lower teeth
- **Open bite.** Front teeth don't meet when biting down

Severe orthodontic or jaw issues may require specialist treatment. A consultation with a certified Invisalign provider is the best way to determine suitability.

## Invisalign vs traditional braces

| Factor | Invisalign | Traditional braces |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Visibility | Nearly invisible | Visible metal or ceramic brackets |
| Removable | Yes, for eating and brushing | No, fixed throughout treatment |
| Comfort | Smooth plastic, less irritation | Brackets and wires can irritate cheeks |
| Treatment time | 6 to 18 months (mild to complex) | 12 to 24 months (mild to complex) |
| Food restrictions | None (aligners removed to eat) | Avoid hard, sticky, and chewy foods |
| Cost (UK) | £2,500 – £5,500 | £1,500 – £6,000 |
| Check up frequency | Every 6 to 8 weeks | Every 4 to 6 weeks |
| Best for | Mild to moderate cases, adults | Complex cases, all age groups |

Both options can deliver excellent results. Invisalign is popular with adults seeking a discreet and convenient solution. Traditional braces may be more effective for complex cases and are sometimes more affordable.

If you've had teeth removed and need replacements, [dental implants](/treatments/dental-implants) are a separate treatment that can be combined with orthodontics.

## What to expect during treatment

**Week 1:**
Mild tightness or pressure is normal. Speech may feel slightly different for the first few days but this resolves quickly.

**Months 1 to 3:**
You settle into a routine. Visible changes begin. Regular check ups ensure everything is on track.

**Mid treatment:**
Refinement aligners may be ordered if adjustments are needed. Your dentist takes new scans to fine tune progress.

**After treatment:**
Retainers are essential to maintain alignment. Most dentists recommend wearing a removable retainer every night indefinitely and may also place a thin bonded wire retainer behind your front teeth.

## How to choose the right Invisalign provider

When comparing clinics, look for:

- **Invisalign provider tier.** Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, and Diamond. Higher tiers indicate more experience with Invisalign cases.
- **Digital scanning technology.** Clinics with iTero scanners offer more precise treatment planning and visualisation.
- **Transparent pricing.** The quote should include all aligners, attachments, retainers, and follow up visits.
- **Before and after case examples.** Ask to see results from cases similar to yours.
- **Invisalign specific reviews.** Look for consistent positive feedback about clear aligner treatment, not just general dentistry.
- **Clear refinement policy.** Understand what happens if additional aligners are needed.
