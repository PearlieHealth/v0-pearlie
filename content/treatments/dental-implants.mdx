## What are dental implants?

Dental implants are the gold standard for replacing missing teeth. An implant consists of three parts: a small titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone, an abutment (connector) that attaches to the post, and a custom-made crown that looks and functions like a natural tooth.

Unlike bridges or dentures, implants replace the entire tooth — including the root. This means they prevent bone loss in the jaw (a common consequence of missing teeth), feel completely natural, and can last a lifetime with proper care.

## Types of dental implants

There are several implant options depending on how many teeth you need to replace:

**Single implant**
Replaces one missing tooth with one titanium post and one crown. This is the most common type and costs £2,000–£3,500.

**Implant-supported bridge**
Replaces 3–4 adjacent missing teeth using 2 implant posts supporting a bridge. This is more cost-effective than placing individual implants for each tooth.

**All-on-4 / All-on-6**
A full arch of teeth (upper or lower jaw) supported by 4 or 6 strategically placed implants. This is the go-to solution for patients who have lost most or all teeth in an arch. Costs £8,000–£15,000 per arch.

**Zygomatic implants**
Longer implants that anchor into the cheekbone (zygoma) rather than the jawbone. Used for patients with severe jawbone loss who cannot have standard implants or bone grafts. This is a specialist procedure offered at fewer clinics.

## The dental implant procedure step by step

**Step 1: Consultation and assessment**
Your implant dentist takes a 3D CT scan to assess your jawbone density, plan the implant position, and identify any need for bone grafting. You receive a detailed treatment plan with costs.

**Step 2: Bone grafting (if needed)**
If your jawbone is insufficient, a bone graft is performed to build up the bone. This adds 3–6 months to the timeline as the graft needs to heal and integrate before the implant can be placed.

**Step 3: Implant placement**
The titanium post is surgically placed into the jawbone under local anaesthetic. The procedure typically takes 1–2 hours per implant. A temporary tooth or healing cap is placed over the implant.

**Step 4: Osseointegration (healing)**
Over 2–6 months, the implant fuses with the surrounding bone in a process called osseointegration. During this time, you may wear a temporary crown or denture.

**Step 5: Abutment and crown placement**
Once the implant has fully integrated, the abutment is attached and impressions are taken for your permanent crown. The final crown is matched to your natural teeth in colour and shape.

## Who is a good candidate for dental implants?

Dental implants are suitable for most adults with good general health. Key requirements include:

- **Adequate jawbone density** — enough bone to support the implant post (bone grafting can help if not)
- **Healthy gums** — active gum disease must be treated before implant placement
- **Non-smoker or willing to quit** — smoking significantly increases implant failure rates
- **Good general health** — uncontrolled diabetes and certain medications can affect healing
- **Commitment to oral hygiene** — implants require the same care as natural teeth

Age is not a barrier — implants are placed successfully in patients from their late teens through to their 80s and beyond.

## Recovery and aftercare

**Days 1–3:** Mild swelling, bruising, and discomfort are normal. Apply ice packs and take prescribed painkillers. Eat soft foods only.

**Week 1:** Swelling subsides. Continue with soft foods. Avoid the implant area when brushing — rinse gently with warm salt water.

**Weeks 2–6:** Return to normal eating gradually. The area continues to heal around the implant.

**Months 2–6:** Osseointegration (the implant fusing with bone). Avoid putting excessive pressure on the implant. Your dentist monitors healing at scheduled check-ups.

**Long term:** Once the final crown is placed, care for your implant like a natural tooth — brush twice daily, floss, and attend regular dental check-ups.

## How to choose the right implant clinic

When selecting an implant dentist, consider:

- **Qualifications and experience** — look for dentists with postgraduate training in implantology (e.g., Diploma/MSc in Implant Dentistry)
- **Technology** — clinics with CBCT scanners, digital planning, and guided surgery offer more precise results
- **Transparent pricing** — the quote should include all components: implant, abutment, crown, CT scan, and follow-up visits
- **Aftercare guarantee** — many clinics offer a 5–10 year guarantee on implants
- **Patient reviews** — look for feedback specifically about implant cases
- **GDC registration** — all practicing dentists must be registered with the General Dental Council
