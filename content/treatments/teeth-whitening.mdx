## What is professional teeth whitening?

Professional teeth whitening is a cosmetic treatment that lightens the natural colour of your teeth without removing tooth structure.

It works using hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide gel to safely break down stains inside the enamel.

Unlike shop bought kits:

- **Higher (legally regulated) whitening strength** — up to 6% hydrogen peroxide
- **Custom fitted trays** for even coverage and reduced gum irritation
- **Dentist supervision** for safe application and sensitivity management
- **Faster and more predictable results** — typically 2 to 8 shades lighter

In the UK, whitening products containing more than 0.1% hydrogen peroxide can only legally be used by dental professionals.

## Types of professional teeth whitening

**Home whitening (custom trays)**
Custom trays made from digital scans or impressions. Worn daily for 1 to 3 weeks. The most popular and cost effective professional option.

**In-office whitening (Zoom / laser)**
High concentration gel applied in the dental chair and activated with a special light. Single 60 to 90 minute session with immediate visible results.

**Boutique Whitening**
Premium custom tray system with specially formulated gels. Available in day wear and night wear formulas. 1 to 2 week treatment. Known for low sensitivity and natural looking results.

**Enlighten Whitening**
The only whitening system designed to achieve the lightest natural shade (B1). Combines two weeks of home whitening with a final in-office session. Considered the gold standard for dramatic, long lasting results.

## Who is a good candidate?

Whitening works best for:

- **Yellow staining.** Natural yellowing responds very well to whitening
- **Surface staining.** Discolouration from coffee, tea, wine, smoking, or ageing
- **Healthy teeth and gums.** No untreated cavities or active gum disease
- **Adults 18+.** Whitening is not recommended for children or teenagers

Whitening may be less effective for:

- **Intrinsic staining.** Discolouration from within the tooth (tetracycline staining, fluorosis)
- **Grey or brown teeth.** These respond less predictably than yellow teeth
- **Teeth with many crowns or veneers.** Restorations do not change colour

A dental consultation confirms suitability. Many patients choose to whiten their teeth after completing [Invisalign](/treatments/invisalign) treatment for maximum impact.

## Managing sensitivity

Sensitivity is the most common concern with whitening. A good clinic takes proactive steps to minimise it:

- **Pre-treatment desensitising.** Using a potassium nitrate toothpaste (such as Sensodyne) for 2 weeks before whitening reduces nerve sensitivity.
- **Desensitising gel in trays.** Some systems include a separate desensitising gel applied after each whitening session.
- **Concentration adjustment.** Your dentist can recommend a lower concentration gel with longer wear time for sensitivity-prone patients.
- **Day vs night wear.** Day wear gels (typically 30 to 60 minutes) may cause less sensitivity than overnight wear.
- **Post-treatment care.** Avoiding very hot or cold food and drink for 24 to 48 hours after treatment helps.

If you have existing sensitivity or exposed root surfaces, discuss this at your consultation. Professional whitening can almost always be adapted to your comfort level.

## Understanding shade expectations

Not everyone achieves the same result from whitening. Setting realistic expectations is important:

- **Starting shade matters.** Teeth with yellow undertones respond best. Grey, brown, or tetracycline-stained teeth are harder to whiten.
- **The Vita shade guide.** Dentists use this standardised colour chart (ranging from A1 to D4, with B1 being the lightest natural shade) to measure your starting point and track progress.
- **Typical improvement.** Most patients achieve 2 to 8 shades lighter. The degree depends on the method, duration, and your natural tooth composition.
- **Whitening ceiling.** Every person has a maximum whiteness their teeth can achieve. Going beyond this isn't possible with whitening alone.
- **Existing restorations.** Fillings, crowns, and veneers won't change colour. If they're visible in your smile, they may need replacing to match your new shade.

## Whitening after other treatments

Timing matters when combining whitening with other cosmetic or orthodontic treatments:

- **After Invisalign.** Wait 1 to 2 weeks after removing aligners before whitening, to allow enamel to rehydrate. Whitening trays can often be made from your existing Invisalign moulds.
- **Before composite bonding.** Always whiten first, then bond to match your lighter shade. Composite cannot be whitened after placement.
- **Before porcelain veneers.** Whiten your natural teeth to your desired shade before veneer fabrication, so the ceramist can match the veneers to your whitened teeth.
- **After dental implants.** Implant crowns won't change colour. Whiten natural teeth first, then have implant crowns made or replaced to match.

## Maintaining your whitening results

To maintain brightness:

- **Avoid staining foods and drinks** for 48 hours after treatment (coffee, tea, red wine, curry, berries)
- **Use a straw** for dark drinks to minimise contact with teeth
- **Brush twice daily** with a whitening toothpaste
- **Don't smoke.** Tobacco is one of the biggest causes of tooth staining
- **Top up treatments** every 6 to 12 months using your custom home trays
- **Regular dental hygiene visits.** Professional cleaning removes surface staining

With good maintenance and occasional top ups, results can last 6 to 24 months.

When comparing providers, look for:

- **GDC registration.** Essential. Whitening by non registered practitioners is illegal in the UK.
- **Multiple whitening systems available.** Good clinics offer home, in-office, and premium options and recommend the best fit for your needs
- **Transparent pricing.** The quote should include everything: consultation, trays, gel, and follow up
- **Clear sensitivity management.** Proactive desensitising protocols before and after treatment
- **Before and after photos.** Ask to see results from previous patients
