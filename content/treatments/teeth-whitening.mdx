## What is professional teeth whitening?

Professional teeth whitening is a cosmetic dental treatment that lightens the natural colour of your teeth without removing any tooth surface. A dentist applies a controlled concentration of hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide gel to your teeth, which breaks down stains and discolouration.

Unlike shop-bought whitening products, professional treatments use higher (legally regulated) concentrations of whitening agents and are supervised by a qualified dental professional. This means faster, more dramatic, and safer results.

In the UK, teeth whitening can only legally be performed by or under the supervision of a GDC-registered dental professional.

## Types of professional teeth whitening

**Home whitening with custom trays**
Your dentist takes impressions of your teeth and creates custom-fitted trays. You fill the trays with whitening gel and wear them for a set period each day (usually 30 minutes to overnight) for 1–3 weeks. This is the most popular and cost-effective professional option.

**In-office whitening (Zoom / laser)**
A high-concentration whitening gel is applied to your teeth in the dental chair and activated with a special light or laser. Treatment takes 60–90 minutes in a single visit. Results are immediate, though some patients have a top-up with home trays.

**Boutique Whitening**
A premium home whitening system using custom trays and specially formulated gels. Available in day-wear and night-wear formulas. Treatment takes 1–2 weeks. Known for low sensitivity and natural-looking results.

**Enlighten Whitening**
The only whitening system that guarantees a B1 shade (the whitest natural shade). Combines 2 weeks of home whitening with a final in-office treatment. Considered the gold standard for dramatic, long-lasting results.

## How teeth whitening works

Professional whitening uses hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide gel to break down stain molecules trapped in the tooth enamel. Here's the science:

1. **Gel application** — The whitening gel is applied directly to the tooth surface (in-office) or placed in custom trays (at-home)
2. **Oxidation** — The active ingredient penetrates the enamel and breaks down chromogenic (colour-causing) compounds through oxidation
3. **Stain removal** — Discolouration from coffee, tea, wine, smoking, and ageing is lifted from within the enamel
4. **Result** — Teeth appear lighter and brighter, typically 2–8 shades depending on the treatment

Professional treatments use legally regulated concentrations of hydrogen peroxide (up to 6% in the UK) for safe, effective results.

## Who is a good candidate for teeth whitening?

Teeth whitening works best for:

- **Extrinsic staining** — discolouration from food, drink, smoking, or ageing
- **Yellowed teeth** — natural yellowing responds very well to whitening
- **Healthy teeth and gums** — no untreated cavities or active gum disease
- **Adults 18+** — whitening is not recommended for children or teenagers

Whitening may be less effective for:

- **Intrinsic staining** — discolouration from within the tooth (tetracycline staining, fluorosis)
- **Grey or brown teeth** — these respond less predictably than yellow teeth
- **Patients with extensive restorations** — crowns, veneers, and fillings don't whiten

A consultation with your dentist will determine if whitening is right for you and which method will give the best results.

## Professional vs over-the-counter whitening

| Factor | Professional | Over-the-counter |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Active ingredient strength | Up to 6% hydrogen peroxide | Up to 0.1% hydrogen peroxide |
| Custom-fitted trays | Yes | No (one-size-fits-all) |
| Results | 2–8 shades lighter | 1–2 shades lighter |
| Speed | 1–3 weeks (or 1 visit) | 4–8 weeks |
| Safety | Dentist-supervised | Self-administered |
| Cost | £250 – £1,000 | £20 – £80 |
| Longevity | 6 months – 2 years | 1–3 months |

The key difference is the concentration of whitening agent. UK law restricts products containing more than 0.1% hydrogen peroxide to dental professionals. Over-the-counter products cannot legally contain effective concentrations, which is why professional results are dramatically better.

## Maintaining your whitening results

To keep your teeth looking their best after whitening:

- **Avoid staining foods and drinks** for 48 hours after treatment (coffee, tea, red wine, curry, berries)
- **Use a straw** for dark drinks to minimise contact with teeth
- **Brush twice daily** with a whitening toothpaste
- **Don't smoke** — tobacco is one of the biggest causes of tooth staining
- **Top-up treatments** every 6–12 months using your custom home trays
- **Regular dental hygiene visits** — professional cleaning removes surface staining

Most patients find that with good habits and occasional top-ups, their whitening results last well over a year.

## How to choose the right whitening clinic

When comparing whitening providers:

- **GDC registration** — essential. Whitening by non-registered practitioners is illegal in the UK.
- **Treatment options** — good clinics offer multiple methods (home, in-office, Enlighten) and recommend the best option for your needs
- **Transparent pricing** — the quote should include everything: consultation, trays, gel, and follow-up
- **Before-and-after photos** — ask to see results from previous patients
- **Patient reviews** — look for feedback about whitening specifically
- **Sensitivity management** — good clinics proactively manage sensitivity with desensitising protocols
