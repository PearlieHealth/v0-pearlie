## What is professional teeth whitening?

Professional teeth whitening is a cosmetic treatment that lightens the natural colour of your teeth without removing tooth structure.

It works using hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide gel to safely break down stains inside the enamel.

Unlike shop bought kits:

- **Higher (legally regulated) whitening strength** up to 6% hydrogen peroxide
- **Custom fitted trays** for even coverage and reduced gum irritation
- **Dentist supervision** for safe application and sensitivity management
- **Faster and more predictable results** typically 2 to 8 shades lighter

In the UK, whitening products containing more than 0.1% hydrogen peroxide can only legally be used by dental professionals.

## Types of professional teeth whitening

**Home whitening (custom trays)**
Custom trays made from digital scans or impressions. Worn daily for 1 to 3 weeks. The most popular and cost effective professional option.

**In office whitening (Zoom / laser)**
High concentration gel applied in the dental chair and activated with a special light. Single 60 to 90 minute session with immediate visible results.

**Boutique Whitening**
Premium custom tray system with specially formulated gels. Available in day wear and night wear formulas. 1 to 2 week treatment. Known for low sensitivity and natural looking results.

**Enlighten Whitening**
The only whitening system designed to achieve the lightest natural shade (B1). Combines two weeks of home whitening with a final in office session. Considered the gold standard for dramatic, long lasting results.

## How teeth whitening works

Professional whitening uses peroxide gel to break down stain molecules trapped within the tooth enamel:

1. **Gel application.** Applied directly to the tooth surface (in office) or placed in custom trays (at home)
2. **Oxidation.** The active ingredient penetrates the enamel and breaks down chromogenic (colour causing) compounds
3. **Stain removal.** Discolouration from coffee, tea, wine, smoking, and ageing is lifted from within the enamel
4. **Result.** Teeth appear lighter and brighter, typically 2 to 8 shades depending on the treatment

Professional treatments use legally regulated concentrations of hydrogen peroxide (up to 6% in the UK) for safe, effective results.

## Who is a good candidate?

Whitening works best for:

- **Yellow staining.** Natural yellowing responds very well to whitening
- **Surface staining.** Discolouration from coffee, tea, wine, smoking, or ageing
- **Healthy teeth and gums.** No untreated cavities or active gum disease
- **Adults 18+.** Whitening is not recommended for children or teenagers

Whitening may be less effective for:

- **Intrinsic staining.** Discolouration from within the tooth (tetracycline staining, fluorosis)
- **Grey or brown teeth.** These respond less predictably than yellow teeth
- **Teeth with many crowns or veneers.** Restorations do not change colour

A dental consultation confirms suitability. Many patients choose to whiten their teeth after completing [Invisalign](/treatments/invisalign) treatment for maximum impact.

## Maintaining your whitening results

To maintain brightness:

- **Avoid staining foods and drinks** for 48 hours after treatment (coffee, tea, red wine, curry, berries)
- **Use a straw** for dark drinks to minimise contact with teeth
- **Brush twice daily** with a whitening toothpaste
- **Don't smoke.** Tobacco is one of the biggest causes of tooth staining
- **Top up treatments** every 6 to 12 months using your custom home trays
- **Regular dental hygiene visits.** Professional cleaning removes surface staining

With good maintenance and occasional top ups, results can last 6 to 24 months.
